# Methodology

## Objective

The objective of this research was to identify and evaluate Pune-based manufacturing companies that fit the DT Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) using the Federer Framework. The focus was on finding promoter-led Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) that demonstrate strong leadership, operational excellence, growth potential, and long-term sustainability.

---

## Target Geography

### Selected City

**Pune, Maharashtra**

### Reason for Selection

Pune was selected because it is one of India's largest manufacturing hubs and contains a dense cluster of:

* Automotive component manufacturers
* Precision engineering companies
* Industrial automation firms
* Tooling and machining businesses
* Aerospace and defense suppliers
* Sheet metal and fabrication companies

The city offers a large concentration of founder-led SMEs within DT's preferred revenue range, making it an ideal location for research.

## Research Process

### Step 1: Company Discovery

A broad list of manufacturing companies operating in Pune was created.

Searches included:

* Pune precision engineering companies
* Pune automotive component manufacturers
* Pune industrial automation companies
* Pune tooling companies
* Pune CNC machining companies
* Pune sheet metal manufacturers

This produced an initial list of more than 50 companies.

---

### Step 2: E1 Producer Filter

The first filter applied was the Producer Check.

Question:
**Does the company manufacture products itself?**

Companies were accepted only if there was clear evidence of:

* Manufacturing facilities
* Production machinery
* Factory operations
* In-house production capabilities

Companies acting only as:

* Traders
* Dealers
* Distributors
* Service providers

were rejected.

---

### Step 3: E2 Accessibility Filter

The second filter checked whether the company was operationally accessible.

Requirements:

* Active operations in India
* Identifiable decision makers
* Reachable management structure
* Operational manufacturing facilities

Companies without sufficient accessibility or operational visibility were removed.

---

### Step 4: Federer Framework Evaluation

Each company was evaluated using the Federer Framework.

### C3 – Differentiation

Assessment Criteria:

* Proprietary technology
* Unique manufacturing capabilities
* Specialized products
* Competitive advantages

Examples:

* Proprietary feeder technology
* Integrated manufacturing capabilities
* Export-grade certifications

---

### C4 – Decision Maker Quality

Assessment Criteria:

* Founder involvement
* Technical expertise
* Leadership experience
* Strategic vision

Evidence included:

* Founder biographies
* Leadership interviews
* Management experience
* Business growth history

---

### C5 – Growing Sector

Assessment Criteria:

* Industry growth potential
* Market demand
* Long-term industry trends

High-growth sectors included:

* Industrial automation
* Electric vehicles
* Aerospace
* Precision engineering
* Advanced manufacturing

---

### C6 – Growth Signals

Assessment Criteria:

* Plant expansion
* Export growth
* Hiring activity
* New product launches
* Market expansion

Evidence was collected from:

* Company news
* Career pages
* Press releases
* Financial information

---

### C7 – Systems Maturity

Assessment Criteria:

* ERP implementation
* ISO certifications
* Quality systems
* Advanced manufacturing infrastructure

Indicators included:

* ISO 9001
* IATF 16949
* SAP/ERP systems
* Automated production systems

---

### C8 – Leadership Succession

Assessment Criteria:

* Family continuity
* Next-generation involvement
* Professional management
* Long-term sustainability

Companies with visible succession planning received higher scores.

---

## Scoring Method

Each company was scored using the Federer Framework.

Score Categories:

### Strong

* Significant evidence
* Clear competitive advantage
* Strong business fundamentals

### Moderate

* Some evidence available
* Good potential
* Partial validation

### Weak

* Limited evidence
* Insufficient differentiation
* Weak indicators

The final score determined the overall Federer classification.

## Key Findings

The strongest Federer companies shared common characteristics:

### 1. Founder-Led Leadership

The highest-scoring companies were led by founders or second-generation entrepreneurs.

### 2. Export Orientation

Many strong candidates exported products internationally.

### 3. Manufacturing Differentiation

Top companies possessed specialized technologies or integrated manufacturing capabilities.

### 4. Systems Discipline

Successful companies invested heavily in quality systems, certifications, and process maturity.

### 5. Leadership Continuity

Many strong candidates demonstrated active second-generation involvement.

---

## Conclusion

Through a structured research process involving multiple data sources, E1/E2 qualification filters, and the Federer Framework, a final list of 25 qualified manufacturing companies was developed. These companies represent DT's preferred profile of promoter-led, growth-oriented manufacturing SMEs capable of benefiting from structured execution systems and long-term organizational development.
